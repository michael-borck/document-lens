# Helios Energy Group — Annual Report 2022

## Chairman's letter

This year the board approved the most significant reshaping of the Helios portfolio in a generation. The Kestrel Point battery — 250 megawatts, four hours of storage — received final investment approval in June, and construction began in October. It is the first of what we expect to be a series of renewable energy initiatives, and it marks the point at which decarbonisation moved from our risk register into our capital plan.

Group revenue reached $4.7 billion and underlying earnings $701 million. The dividend rose to 46 cents per share.

## Operating review

Generation output of 39.1 terawatt hours was delivered at 92 per cent availability, meeting target for the first time in four years. The retail division passed two million accounts. Wholesale volatility again favoured our trading desk, though we note that the same volatility is precisely what the Kestrel Point battery is designed to monetise more systematically.

Capital expenditure rose to $520 million, of which $180 million related to the battery and associated grid connection. The remainder funded station reliability and the second stage of the energy efficiency program, which this year delivered 52 gigawatt hours of avoided auxiliary load — ahead of forecast.

## Climate and environment

Our carbon reduction strategies now rest on three funded programs: the storage build, the auxiliary energy efficiency work, and a fuel-blending trial at Wattle Creek that substitutes up to 15 per cent biomass. Together these underpin our target of a 12 per cent emissions reduction in operational intensity by 2025; at year end we were tracking at 7 per cent below baseline, ahead of the glide path.

Beyond emissions reduction, environmental reporting matured considerably this year. We published our first standalone data supplement, covering greenhouse intensity, water withdrawals, and ash reuse, with independent limited assurance over the greenhouse figures. A water conservation program at our two inland stations cut freshwater withdrawals by 9 per cent, principally through condenser cycle improvements. The Wattle Creek waste reduction program expanded to 21,000 tonnes of ash diverted into road-base and cement supply.

## Our people and communities

The workforce development program with the regional technical college doubled to 80 apprenticeship places, and we funded a new high-voltage training facility on the college campus. Community partnerships around our station towns — long informal — were placed on a structured footing, with three-year funding agreements for the Kestrel Point community trust and the Wattle Creek sporting precinct.

Safety performance improved, with the total recordable injury rate falling from 5.8 to 4.6 per million hours. Mental health support services were extended to contractors on the battery construction site.

## Outlook

The 2023 agenda is delivery: commission the battery on schedule, hold station availability at target, and keep the emissions reduction program ahead of its glide path. The board will also consider a second storage site and the first stage of the transmission-connected solar study. The transition is no longer an abstraction for Helios; it is a construction schedule.

Gerald Havelock, Chairman
