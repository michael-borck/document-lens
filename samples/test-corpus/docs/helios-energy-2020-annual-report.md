# Helios Energy Group — Annual Report 2020

## Chairman's letter

The 2020 financial year tested Helios Energy Group in ways few of us anticipated. Demand across our generation portfolio fell sharply in the second half, wholesale prices remained volatile, and the maintenance backlog at the Kestrel Point plant absorbed a larger share of capital than planned. Against that backdrop, the board's priority was straightforward: protect the balance sheet, keep the plants running safely, and preserve dividends for the shareholders who have backed this company for four decades.

Group revenue came in at $4.1 billion, down 6 per cent on the prior year. Underlying earnings held at $612 million, a result that reflects disciplined cost-cutting measures across every operating division. We renegotiated fuel supply contracts, consolidated our regional offices from eleven sites to seven, and completed the outsourcing of facilities maintenance and payroll administration to specialist providers. These decisions were not easy, and I acknowledge the staff who left the business during the year. They were necessary to keep Helios competitive.

## Operating review

Generation output totalled 38.2 terawatt hours across our six thermal stations and two gas peaking plants. Availability averaged 89 per cent, below our 92 per cent target, largely due to the unplanned turbine outage at Kestrel Point in March. The repair program was completed in September, on budget, and the unit returned to full dispatch in October.

Our retail division added 84,000 customer accounts, driven by competitive pricing in the small business segment. Churn remains the key challenge; the market continues to reward aggressive discounting, and we will not chase unprofitable volume. The trading desk delivered another strong year, capturing value from winter price spikes in the southern market.

Capital expenditure of $310 million was directed principally at plant reliability: boiler retubing at Wattle Creek, control system upgrades at two stations, and the completion of the ash dam expansion approved in 2018. A modest energy efficiency program in our office portfolio reduced corporate electricity costs by 4 per cent, and further procurement savings are expected next year.

## Financial position

Net debt closed at $2.6 billion, with gearing at 34 per cent — comfortably within covenant thresholds. The refinancing of the 2021 bond series was completed in November at favourable margins. The board declared a final dividend of 22 cents per share, taking the full-year distribution to 41 cents, fully franked.

The economic impact of the pandemic on industrial demand remains the principal uncertainty for 2021. Our planning assumes a slow recovery through the first half, with load returning to trend by December. We have stress-tested the portfolio against a slower scenario and are confident the company can sustain its distribution policy in either case.

## Outlook

The energy market is changing, and Helios will change with it — at the pace our shareholders' capital deserves, and no faster. The immediate agenda for 2021 is operational: lift availability above target, complete the second stage of the cost program, and finish the enterprise resource planning consolidation now that the outsourcing transition has settled. We enter the new year with a strong order book, a proven management team, and a clear mandate from the board.

Gerald Havelock, Chairman
