# Helios Energy Group — Annual Report 2023

## Chair's letter

The Kestrel Point battery entered commercial operation in August, three weeks early and 4 per cent under budget. It is already reshaping how we dispatch the adjacent plant, and it has changed how this company thinks about itself. This year's report is organised around the pillars of our new climate strategy, adopted by the board in February, which commits Helios to net zero operational emissions by 2045 with an interim 30 per cent emissions reduction by 2030.

Group revenue reached $5.0 billion, underlying earnings $748 million, and the dividend 48 cents per share. Growth and transition are proving to be compatible.

## Energy transition

Following the battery's success, the board approved a second storage project at Wattle Creek and committed development capital to a 400 megawatt solar precinct on rehabilitated mine land. These renewable energy initiatives sit alongside continuing carbon reduction strategies in the existing fleet: the biomass co-firing trial reached 18 per cent substitution, and stage three of the energy efficiency program lifted cumulative auxiliary savings to 78 gigawatt hours a year. Sustainable energy use is now a formal criterion in all procurement above $5 million, embedded in our procurement policy.

At year end, operational greenhouse intensity stood 11 per cent below the 2020 baseline — within reach of the 2025 target two years early. Environmental reporting expanded to full value-chain emissions for the first time, with independent assurance extended to scope 2.

## Environment and resources

The water conservation program delivered a further 6 per cent reduction in freshwater withdrawals, and water quality monitoring was upgraded at all inland stations with continuous telemetry replacing monthly sampling. Waste reduction reached 34,000 tonnes of ash diverted, and the road-base partnership was extended for five years. On rehabilitated land at the former Merrigal mine we began a biodiversity conservation program with the state herbarium, re-establishing endangered grassland species across 400 hectares — an exercise in practical environmental stewardship that also derisks the solar precinct approvals.

## People and communities

Our first Gender equity action plan was published in March: targets for women in leadership at every level, pay-gap disclosure, and structured sponsorship. Women now hold 31 per cent of senior roles, up from 26 per cent. A broader diversity and inclusion council, chaired by the chief executive, meets quarterly, and disability inclusion audits were completed at all corporate sites.

The workforce development program placed its two-hundredth apprentice. Health and wellbeing investment rose 40 per cent, with mental health support extended to families of shift workers. Community partnerships now operate in every station town, and the community trusts distributed $3.1 million during the year.

## Governance and outlook

Climate risk oversight moved from the audit committee to a dedicated board committee, and executive remuneration is now linked to the emissions reduction glide path. Our climate resilience assessment — covering flood, heat, and water stress across all sites — was completed and will drive adaptation spending from 2024.

The 2024 agenda: deliver the second battery, reach financial close on the solar precinct, and hold the transition to schedule. The direction is set.

Miriam Chao, Chair
