# Meridian Regional Bank — Annual Report 2023

## Chief Executive's review

This was a year of margin pressure and hard choices. Cash earnings of $139 million were 10 per cent below the prior year as deposit competition intensified and funding costs rose faster than we could reprice the book. The board's response has been a comprehensive productivity program, and much of this report necessarily deals with it.

## Resetting the cost base

The productivity program targets $38 million of annualised savings by the end of 2025. Its main elements are the consolidation of three operations centres into one, renegotiation of our two largest technology contracts, automation of manual back-office processing, and a managed reduction of around 140 roles, achieved so far mainly through attrition. We have also introduced market segmentation across the deposit base so that pricing better reflects the cost to serve, and premium service tiers for high-balance customers whose economics support dedicated relationship management.

These are commercial decisions and we make no apology for them. Revenue maximisation across the existing customer base — deepening wallet share rather than chasing new markets — is the strategy the board has endorsed for the next three years. What we have not done is close branches: the network guarantee holds through 2026.

## Customers

Hardship volumes rose again as fixed-rate loans reset; we assisted 3,900 households experiencing financial hardship, and the fee-waiver program continued. Community support grants were maintained at $1.9 million, though the discretionary sponsorship budget was reduced as part of the productivity program. Basic no-fee accounts reached 71,000.

The rebuilt mortgage origination platform halved approval times, and the digital transformation program entered its final stage with the payments migration scheduled for completion next year.

## Our people

Staff numbers ended the year at 1,750. The role reductions were handled with extended notice periods, redeployment support, and funded retraining; even so, the engagement survey fell six points, and rebuilding it is a stated management objective for 2024. Women in leadership held at 45 per cent of executive roles. The workforce development budget was maintained for accreditation-critical training, with discretionary programs deferred.

## Governance and outlook

The customer outcomes committee reviewed the market segmentation and premium service tiers changes before launch, and its conditions — no degradation of service standards for basic accounts, annual review of fee outcomes for low-balance customers — are binding on management. Governance of the productivity program sits with a board subcommittee meeting monthly.

We expect 2024 earnings to stabilise as the savings build. The franchise commitments — the branch guarantee, hardship capacity, community support — remain funded. Everything else must earn its place.

Ruth Calloway, Chief Executive
