# Helios Energy Group — Annual Report 2021

## Chairman's letter

After the disruptions of 2020, this was a year of consolidation and, in places, cautious renewal. Group revenue recovered to $4.4 billion and underlying earnings rose to $655 million. The cost program launched last year concluded ahead of schedule, and while some cost-cutting measures remain in place across corporate functions, the heavy restructuring is behind us.

The board has also begun to engage seriously with the questions our investors are now asking at every meeting: how the portfolio performs as markets decarbonise, and what that means for capital allocation over the coming decade. This report includes, for the first time, a short section on environmental performance. It is a modest start, and we intend to build on it.

## Operating review

Generation output rose to 39.6 terawatt hours with availability of 91 per cent, close to target. The retail division grew to 1.9 million accounts, and the trading business again performed strongly in a volatile wholesale market.

Capital expenditure of $340 million included the completion of the Wattle Creek reliability program and the first tranche of our grid-scale battery study at Kestrel Point. The study will inform a board decision in 2022 on whether storage has a commercial role in our dispatch profile.

## Environmental performance

Two initiatives moved from planning into delivery this year. First, the energy efficiency program that began in our office portfolio was extended to plant auxiliary systems; upgraded pumps and variable-speed drives at three stations are expected to save 45 gigawatt hours annually. Energy efficiency is now a standing item in each station's maintenance plan.

Second, we set our first group-wide target for emissions reduction: a 12 per cent cut in operational greenhouse intensity by 2025, against a 2020 baseline. The target is unambitious by the standards of some peers, but it is funded, measurable, and owned by line management rather than a sustainability office. A waste reduction trial at Wattle Creek diverted 8,000 tonnes of ash by-product into road-base supply, and we are negotiating offtake agreements to expand this.

## Our people

Following two years of workforce contraction, hiring resumed in engineering and trades. A workforce development partnership with the regional technical college placed 40 apprentices across our stations, the largest intake since 2014. Staff turnover fell to 11 per cent, and the annual engagement survey recorded its first improvement in three years.

## Financial position and outlook

Net debt reduced to $2.4 billion and gearing to 31 per cent. The full-year dividend of 44 cents per share was fully franked. For 2022, we expect earnings growth in the low single digits, with the principal variables being wholesale prices and the pace of industrial demand recovery.

The direction of the market is no longer in serious dispute, and the task for Helios is to navigate the transition without destroying the value our generation assets still produce. The battery study, the emissions reduction target, and the extension of the energy efficiency program are first steps. There will be more to report next year.

Gerald Havelock, Chairman
