# Helios Energy Group — Annual Report 2024

## Chair's letter

Four years ago this company measured itself almost entirely in terawatt hours and cents per share. Those measures still matter — revenue of $5.3 billion, underlying earnings of $792 million, a dividend of 50 cents — but they now sit inside a broader account of performance: our climate strategy glide path, our people, and the communities and ecosystems our operations touch. This year we met our 2025 target for emissions reduction a full year early, reached financial close on the Merrigal solar precinct, and published our first integrated report combining financial and sustainability disclosure in a single volume.

## Energy transition

Operational greenhouse intensity ended the year 13 per cent below the 2020 baseline, beating the 12 per cent target twelve months ahead of schedule. The board has now adopted a deeper interim goal — 35 per cent emissions reduction by 2030 — on the path to carbon neutrality across operations by 2045.

The Wattle Creek battery entered commissioning in November. The Merrigal solar precinct, our largest single investment in renewable energy initiatives to date, begins construction in March. Feasibility work started on green infrastructure at two further sites, including grid-forming inverters and a hydrogen-ready peaking study. Across the existing fleet, carbon reduction strategies delivered again: biomass co-firing reached 21 per cent and the energy efficiency program lifted cumulative savings to 94 gigawatt hours. Sustainable energy use requirements now bind every major supplier through our procurement policy, and environmental reporting achieved reasonable assurance over scope 1 and 2 — a first for our sector locally.

## Environment and resources

Climate resilience adaptation spending began in earnest: flood levees at two stations, heat-rated switchgear, and revised water security agreements. The water conservation program delivered a cumulative 17 per cent reduction in freshwater withdrawals against 2020, verified through expanded water quality monitoring telemetry. Waste reduction reached 41,000 tonnes of ash diverted — 62 per cent of total production — and a circular economy review identified reuse pathways for turbine oils and scrap conductor. The Merrigal biodiversity conservation program passed 900 hectares of grassland restoration, and our environmental stewardship partnership with the state herbarium was extended to include two coastal easements, with early ecological restoration work under way at both.

## People and communities

Women in leadership reached 34 per cent of senior roles. The gender equity plan's pay-gap disclosure showed the median gap narrowing from 8.1 to 6.4 per cent. Diversity and inclusion programs extended to recruitment panels, and the disability inclusion audit recommendations were fully implemented. An Indigenous engagement framework — co-designed with traditional owner corporations at three sites — commits us to cultural heritage co-management and a 3 per cent Indigenous employment target.

Workforce development remains the deepest of our social investments: 260 apprentices placed to date, a new transmission trades stream, and lifelong learning accounts for operational staff facing technology change. Health and wellbeing programs recorded their highest engagement yet, and mental health support utilisation — a measure we publish deliberately — rose 22 per cent as stigma continues to fall. Community partnerships distributed $3.8 million, and community support during the January floods included crews, generators, and accommodation for displaced families.

## Partnerships and governance

The transition cannot be delivered alone. Strategic partnerships signed this year include a storage technology alliance with two universities, an international collaboration on biomass certification with Nordic utilities, and cross-sector alliances on transmission corridor planning with agriculture and telecommunications peers. We joined the national capacity building program for First Nations energy businesses as a founding funder. Policy transparency remains a commitment: all submissions to market bodies are published within 30 days.

Ethical leadership and disciplined capital allocation are not in tension — this year proved it. The 2025 agenda: deliver Merrigal, hold the glide path, and keep faith with the towns that host us.

Miriam Chao, Chair
