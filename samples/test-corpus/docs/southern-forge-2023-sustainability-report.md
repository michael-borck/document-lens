# Southern Forge Industries — Sustainability Report 2023

## Introduction from the Chief Executive

This report covers the environmental and social performance of our four plants for calendar 2023. Where we met a target, the data is here; where we missed one, the data is also here, with what we are doing about it. Our external assurance statement covers every figure in the summary table.

## Climate and energy

Scope 1 and 2 emissions fell 9.2 per cent to 412,000 tonnes, against a target of 8 per cent. The largest contribution came from the electric induction furnace at Corio, which displaced gas-fired melting for the light casting lines. Emissions reduction remains our central environmental commitment: the 2030 target is a 45 per cent cut against 2019, validated against sector decarbonisation pathways.

Energy efficiency projects delivered 31 gigawatt hours of savings — compressed air overhaul at two plants, waste-heat recovery on the Corio annealing line, and high-efficiency motor replacements completed at all sites. A power purchase agreement signed in August will supply 60 per cent of group electricity from wind generation from 2025. Our climate strategy and transition costings appear in full on pages 18 to 24, including the capital schedule per plant.

## Water and land

Freshwater withdrawal fell 11 per cent following the closed-loop quench upgrade at Tarrawin. Water conservation is material for two of our sites in stressed catchments; both now operate under board-approved water budgets with monthly review. Continuous water quality monitoring covers all discharge points, with results published quarterly on our website — including the February exceedance at Corio, its cause (a failed dosing pump), and the remediation completed in March.

At Tarrawin we hold 310 hectares of buffer land. A ten-year land management plan agreed with the catchment authority commits us to weed control, revegetation of 45 hectares, and protection of the remnant grassy woodland; biodiversity conservation outcomes are audited by an independent ecologist every two years. The first ecological restoration stage — 12 hectares along Sandy Creek — was planted in winter with a survival rate of 84 per cent.

## Materials and waste

Waste reduction achieved 76 per cent diversion from landfill, short of our 80 per cent target; foundry sand reuse stalled while the road-authority approval for spent sand in embankment fill was delayed. The approval was granted in November and we expect to recover the shortfall in 2024. Our circular economy work went beyond sand: scrap steel content in castings reached 91 per cent, and take-back trials with two customers returned 1,400 tonnes of end-of-life product for remelt. Environmental reporting now follows the sector guideline in full, and our procurement policy requires recycled content declarations from all metal suppliers.

## People

The total recordable injury rate fell from 6.1 to 4.9 per million hours. Health and wellbeing investment focused on airborne contaminant controls: new fume extraction at two fettling shops, real-time exposure monitoring, and annual lung screening for all foundry staff. Mental health support includes an on-site clinician at each plant one day per week.

Gender equity progress was real but slow: women now hold 24 per cent of leadership roles, up from 21 per cent, and the pay-gap analysis is published unadjusted. Our apprentice intake of 45 included 14 women, the highest proportion in company history. Workforce development spending rose 15 per cent, with a new metallurgy cadetship run jointly with the regional university, and employment outcomes for completing apprentices remain strong: 92 per cent in trade employment twelve months after finishing.

Disability inclusion audits at two plants produced 31 modifications, all completed. An Indigenous engagement protocol governs earthworks at Tarrawin, developed with the traditional owner corporation, and includes cultural monitoring during the restoration planting.

## Working with others

Industry collaboration this year centred on the foundry decarbonisation consortium: shared trials of induction melting parameters, published openly. A technology transfer agreement with the university's materials school is commercialising the low-carbon binder developed in the cadetship program. We also contributed exposure data to the national health research study on foundry particulates — the kind of unglamorous cooperation that moves an industry.

## Assurance and governance

Limited assurance covers all emissions, water, and waste figures. The sustainability committee of the board met five times; its charter, and the management incentives tied to the emissions and safety targets, are set out in the governance section. Institutional integrity is served by publishing our misses alongside our wins — this year, the waste target and the Corio exceedance. Policy transparency: our two submissions to the industrial energy review are on our website in full.

Elena Marchetti, Chief Executive
