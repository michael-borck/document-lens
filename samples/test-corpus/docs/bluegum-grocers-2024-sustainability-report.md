# Bluegum Grocers — Sustainability Report 2024

## A message from our founder

When we opened the first Bluegum store nine years ago, we made one promise: nothing edible goes in the bin. That promise grew into our waste reduction program, and the waste reduction program grew into the sustainability strategy that now shapes every part of how our eight stores run. This report — our second — tells you how we went this year.

## Waste reduction

Waste reduction is where Bluegum started and it is still where we put most of our effort. Across the eight stores we diverted 91 per cent of waste from landfill this year, up from 87 per cent. Unsold edible food goes first to our food-rescue partners — 118 tonnes this year — then to the community fridge program, then to farmers for feed. Our packaging team removed plastic trays from another 40 product lines, and the deli's switch to returnable crates cut cardboard by a third. Next year's waste reduction target is 93 per cent diversion, and the two stores that trialled full organics separation both beat that number already.

## Circular economy

We think a grocer can be a working example of the circular economy at street scale. The jar library — bring one back, take one filled — now runs in every store. The circular economy hub at our Kelso store repairs and re-homes trolleys, crates, and shelving for local market stallholders, and this year it handled 2,900 items. Our compost, made from store organics, went back to eleven of the market gardens we buy from: a small, visible circular economy loop our customers can literally taste. We joined the state circular economy compact as its first small retailer signatory.

## Buying better

Our sustainability strategy sets the rules for what gets on the shelf: local first, seasonal where possible, and suppliers who share the waste reduction ethos. This year the buying team wrote those rules into a formal procurement policy, and 74 per cent of produce spend went to growers within 150 kilometres. The procurement policy also phases out air-freighted produce entirely by 2026.

## Looking ahead

Year two of a written sustainability strategy has taught us that the discipline is in the counting. Every store now has a weekly waste reduction scoreboard by the staff room door, and the numbers drive Monday's ordering. Small retailers don't have sustainability departments — they have habits. Ours are getting better each year, and we will keep publishing the numbers either way.

Tom Nyholm, Founder
