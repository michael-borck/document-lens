# Meridian Regional Bank — Annual Report 2022

## Chief Executive's review

Rising rates restored some margin to banking this year, and Meridian's cash earnings of $155 million reflect that tailwind as much as our own work. Net interest margin expanded to 2.44 per cent. We remained disciplined on costs, generous where it matters, and open in the towns that depend on us — no branch closures for a fifth consecutive year.

## Customers and communities

As rates rose, so did calls to our hardship line. We assisted 3,400 households experiencing financial hardship during the year, extended the fee-waiver program, and doubled our funded places in the financial counselling partnership. The east-coast floods in March hit eleven of our branch towns; community support totalling $2.4 million included emergency grants paid within 48 hours, six months of waived merchant fees for affected businesses, and staff volunteer leave that contributed 3,800 hours to the clean-up.

Access to services expanded through 14 new post-office agencies, and the mobile lending fleet made 900 town visits. Basic no-fee accounts grew to 68,000, and the family-violence banking support program — now co-designed with the legal centre network — assisted 610 customers, with specialist training for all frontline staff dealing with vulnerable populations.

## Our people

Staff numbers held at 1,860. Women in leadership reached 46 per cent of executive roles, and our diversity and inclusion program added a regional First Nations traineeship stream with 15 places. The workforce development budget rose 10 per cent, focused on lending accreditation and cyber skills.

## Operations and environment

The digital transformation program delivered the second stage of core migration, covering the lending book, again without customer outages. The branch energy efficiency retrofit reached 65 sites, and rooftop solar now supplies 9 branches. Our environmental reporting appendix expanded to include financed emissions estimates for the agribusiness book — an early, imperfect number we publish because measurement precedes management.

## Governance and outlook

The customer outcomes committee completed its first full year, reviewing 40 remediation matters; governance disclosures in this report set out each material case and its resolution. Regulatory engagement on the regional banking review absorbed significant board attention, and our submission argued — as we always have — that access to services in small towns is a policy outcome, not a by-product.

The 2023 environment will be harder: margin competition is returning, deposit costs are rising, and technology spend has a long tail. We will protect the franchise investments — branches, hardship capacity, community support — and find efficiency elsewhere. That order of priorities is the point of Meridian.

Ruth Calloway, Chief Executive
